Living in Charlton Kings

A handsome, green and genuinely sought-after suburb on Cheltenham's eastern edge — period homes, outstanding schools and the open hills right on the doorstep. Here's what it's like to live here.

The area guide

A village feel, minutes from town

Charlton Kings is one of Cheltenham's most loved addresses — and it's easy to see why. Tucked beneath the green slopes of Leckhampton and Cleeve Hill, it manages the rare trick of feeling like a proper village while sitting just a few minutes from the heart of a Regency spa town. Tree-lined streets, a friendly high street, independent shops and a real sense of community make it a place people put down roots and rarely leave.

Homes here range from elegant Victorian and Edwardian villas to handsome 1930s semis and a sprinkling of newer, individually designed houses. Whatever the style, gardens tend to be generous, streets are quiet, and the countryside is never more than a short stroll away.

Getting around

Connections are excellent. Cheltenham town centre is around eight minutes by car or a pleasant cycle along the A40, with regular buses running the same route all day. Cheltenham Spa station — direct trains to London Paddington, Bristol, Birmingham and Cardiff — is roughly fifteen minutes away, and the M5 (J11) puts Gloucester, Worcester and the wider South West within easy reach. For everyday life, almost everything you need is walkable right here in the village.

Schools

Schooling is one of the area's biggest draws. Charlton Kings is served by several highly regarded primaries and sits within reach of some of the county's best-rated secondaries and grammar schools, making it a perennial favourite with families who plan to stay for the long term.

Places of interest

Life here is wonderfully unhurried. The village high street is dotted with independent cafés, a butcher, a deli and welcoming pubs, while the open countryside — Leckhampton Hill, the Cotswold Way and Dowdeswell reservoir — is right on the doorstep for weekend walks. Everything Cheltenham offers, from the festivals to the racecourse, is just up the road.

Who lives here

Charlton Kings has a settled, friendly and well-established community — a happy mix of growing families drawn by the schools, professionals commuting to Cheltenham, Gloucester and beyond, and long-standing residents who've enjoyed village life for decades. It's the kind of place where neighbours know each other and people genuinely look out for one another.

The property market

Demand consistently outstrips supply, so well-presented homes tend to sell quickly and hold their value beautifully. Period villas and larger family houses are especially prized, while smartly updated semis and characterful cottages offer a wonderful way into the area. The housing stock is varied, which keeps the village appealing to a broad range of buyers.
